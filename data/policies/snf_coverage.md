# Medicare Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) Coverage

> SOURCE: Medicare Benefit Policy Manual, Ch. 8 (Coverage of Extended Care / SNF
> Services); https://www.cms.gov/regulations-and-guidance/guidance/manuals
> Retrieved 2026-06-29. Summarized/condensed for demonstration; verify against
> the source manual for authoritative text.

## Qualifying conditions
Medicare Part A covers skilled nursing facility care when ALL of the following
conditions are met:

1. The beneficiary has a qualifying inpatient hospital stay of at least three (3)
   consecutive days, not counting the day of discharge.
2. The beneficiary enters a Medicare-certified SNF generally within 30 days of
   hospital discharge.
3. The care requires skilled nursing or skilled rehabilitation services that can
   only be provided on an inpatient basis.
4. The services are for a condition treated during the qualifying hospital stay,
   or that arose while receiving SNF care for that condition.

## Coverage limits
- Days 1–20: covered in full per benefit period.
- Days 21–100: covered subject to a daily coinsurance amount.
- Beyond 100 days: not covered in that benefit period.

## Skilled service requirement
Custodial care alone (help with bathing, dressing, eating) does not qualify. The
need for skilled care must be documented by the physician and the care plan, and
the services must be reasonable and necessary for the patient's condition.
