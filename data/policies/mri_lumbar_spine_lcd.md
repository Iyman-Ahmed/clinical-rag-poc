## Indications (covered conditions)
MRI of the lumbar spine (CPT 72148 without contrast; 72149 with contrast; 72158
without and with contrast) is considered medically necessary for documented
indications including: back/leg pain, sciatica/radiculopathy, degenerative disc
disease, spondylolisthesis, disc herniation (HNP), spinal stenosis, compression
fracture, trauma, and scoliosis.

## Medical necessity — timing rule
Spinal imaging is generally NOT necessary during the first month of symptoms
EXCEPT when a "red flag" (a finding suggesting a medically emergent condition such
as suspected malignancy, infection, cauda equina syndrome, or significant trauma)
is noted on history and physical examination. For a non-red-flag condition, MRI
may be appropriate after approximately one month of symptoms and after an attempt
at conservative management (rest, physical therapy, NSAIDs).

## Documentation requirements
The medical record must document: (1) the specific clinical indication and
relevant signs/symptoms, (2) duration of symptoms and conservative treatment
attempted, and (3) how the imaging result is expected to affect management.

- M51.36 — Intervertebral disc degeneration, lumbar region
