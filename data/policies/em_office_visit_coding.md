# Evaluation and Management (E/M) Office Visit Coding

> SOURCE: AMA CPT E/M guidelines (office/outpatient visits, 2021 revision) and
> CMS E/M documentation guidance;
> https://www.cms.gov/medicare/payment/fee-schedules/physician/evaluation-management-visits
> Retrieved 2026-06-29. Summarized for demonstration; CPT is copyright AMA.

## Office/outpatient visit codes
- **New patient:** 99202–99205.
- **Established patient:** 99211–99215.

Since the 2021 guideline revision, the level of an office/outpatient E/M service
is selected based on EITHER the total time on the date of the encounter OR the
level of medical decision making (MDM). History and exam are no longer used to
determine the code level (though they remain clinically relevant).

## Medical decision making (MDM) elements
MDM level is determined by three components:
1. Number and complexity of problems addressed.
2. Amount and/or complexity of data reviewed and analyzed.
3. Risk of complications, morbidity, or mortality of patient management.

The overall MDM level (straightforward, low, moderate, high) is set by meeting at
least two of the three elements at that level.

## Time-based selection
When selecting by time, count total practitioner time on the date of service:
face-to-face and non-face-to-face work such as reviewing records, ordering tests,
and documentation. Example thresholds: 99214 (established) corresponds to 30–39
minutes total time.

## Documentation
The note must support either the time spent (with a statement of total time) or
the MDM level selected. Billing a level not supported by documentation is a
common source of payment-integrity findings.
